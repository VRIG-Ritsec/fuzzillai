I1 > , /*indirect-value-t*/ < I2 >> && std:: predicate < F & , std:: iter_reference_t < I1 > , std:: iter_reference_t < I2 >> ; (since C++20) The concept indirect_binary_predicate specifies requirements for algorithms that call binary predicates as their arguments. The key difference between this concept and std::predicate is that it is applied to the types that I1 and I2 references, rather than I1 and I2 themselves.
